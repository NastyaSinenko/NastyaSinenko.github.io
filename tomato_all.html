Seed preparation

To always have dill on your table, sow it during the spring and summer at intervals of 10-15 days. The last sowing in open ground is in mid-August. When sowing dry seeds, shoots will appear only after 20-25 days and only under the condition of constant high soil moisture. Slow germination of seeds does not allow for loosening and removing quickly appearing weeds.

Sowing

To ensure germination on the 4th-6th day, the seeds are soaked for 3 days to remove essential oils that prevent germination. Initially, the seeds in a cloth bag are washed for 2-3 minutes in a stream of hot water (60°C), and then placed in water at room temperature for 2-3 days. In this case, the water is changed 2-3 times a day, and the seeds are washed in running cold water. Before sowing, dry the seeds until they are loose.

Little tricks

• do not add ash or lime before sowing, otherwise the dill will turn red and be thin - it likes slightly acidic soil; • do not sow it in shaded areas, where it stretches out, becomes pale, quickly begins to bolt, forming weak umbrellas with flowers; • when sowing, soak the furrows with a solution of mullein (1:10) or vermicompost; • the soil should always be moist to a depth of 15 cm
